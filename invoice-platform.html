01 / Overview

One source of truth for every invoice.

Finance teams were tracking invoices across spreadsheets, email threads, and memory — no shared view of what was approved, paid, overdue, or stuck. The platform brings the entire invoice lifecycle into a single, real-time dashboard with explicit status flows, so anyone can see the state of the business at a glance.

The goal wasn't more features — it was clarity. A product that feels quiet, fast, and trustworthy with money on the line.

02 / The challenge

Spreadsheets don't scale with trust.

As volume grew, the manual process broke down: duplicate entries, unclear ownership, missed due dates, and no audit trail. Reconciling status meant pinging people instead of reading a screen.

The core problems to solve:

  • No shared, real-time view of invoice status.
  • Approval and payment states living in people's heads.
  • Slow, error-prone data entry and search.
  • No confidence in the numbers leadership reported.
03 / Approach

Model the states, then design around them.

I started from the invoice's lifecycle — defining every state (draft, pending, approved, paid, overdue, disputed) and the transitions between them. That state model became the backbone of the IA, the dashboard, and the component system.

From there: a calm visual language (clear typography, restrained color reserved for status), a reusable design system in MUI, and dashboards built around the questions people actually ask.

04 / What it does

Key features

Real-time status flows

Every invoice moves through an explicit, color-coded lifecycle — no guessing where anything stands.

Live dashboard

Totals, aging, and bottlenecks update in real time, so leadership reads the screen instead of asking around.

Fast entry & search

Streamlined forms, smart defaults, and instant filtering turn data entry from a chore into a few keystrokes.

Role-based views

Approvers, finance, and admins each see a focused surface tuned to their job.

Audit trail

Every change is logged and attributable — trust restored to the numbers.

Design system

A reusable MUI component library keeps the product consistent as it grows.
